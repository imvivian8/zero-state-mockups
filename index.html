Opportunity 9 · Q4 FY26

Zero State Mockups

35 ideas across 7 zero-state scenarios on the Marketing Dashboard. Each mockup applies a behavioral-science nudge to convert a dead-end empty state into a next-best action. Click any tile for a full-page mockup.

Scenario 1

No audience created yet

Barrier: Ambiguity aversion + cold-start paralysis

User just signed up. The whole dashboard is blank because no contacts have been imported. Highest-population zero state per Deepak's hunch.

1.A
ICP Sample Dashboard
Show a fully lit-up dashboard with realistic data for their industry. Watermark "Sample data." One CTA: import contacts.
Tactic: Goal visualization
→
1.B
3-Path Low-Commitment Importer
"Start in 30 seconds": paste 5 emails, connect Gmail, or upload CSV. Visible time estimates remove cold-start freeze.
Tactic: Effort reduction
→
1.C
Goal-First Agent Onboarding
Two questions: what do you sell, who buys it. AI builds audience schema + segments + first email. Audience setup as byproduct.
Tactic: Reframing
→
1.D
Borrowed Social Proof Tile
"12,000 nonprofits like you started here. Median time to first send: 14 minutes." Anchors effort estimate.
Tactic: Social proof
→
1.E
Test-With-My-Inbox Trick
Pre-create a tiny audience with just the user's email. They send a real campaign to themselves first, then import.
Tactic: Foot in the door
→

Scenario 7

Sent campaigns but data is 0 / awful

Barrier: Failure framing + no diagnosis

User hits send but sees 0 opens, 14% bounce, or unsubscribe spike. Today: just numbers, no path forward.

7.A
Diagnostic Insight, Not Just Display
"0 opens usually means: subject line, send time, or list health. Your list has 14% bounces — clean it?"
Tactic: Reframe failure
→
7.B
AI A/B Retry Suggestion
"Try the same content with this subject to a 10% holdout." Turns a zero into experiment, not verdict.
Tactic: Growth mindset
→
7.C
List Hygiene CTA
Specific to bounce/unsub spikes. "Your unsub rate is 10% (industry: 0.3%). Re-segment?"
Tactic: Specific diagnosis
→
7.D
Time-of-Send Analyzer
"Sent Tue 3am. Your audience opens most at Thu 10am. Reschedule next send?"
Tactic: Behavioral data
→
7.E
Comparable-Brand Template Swap
"Same-industry brand your size got 32% open rate with this subject. Try this approach?"
Tactic: Lookalike success

Bonus

Beat the industry — benchmark + trajectory

Barrier: No reference point + low goal commitment

Cross-cutting nudge that works on top of any zero state. Replaces the empty widget with a peer benchmark + a visible trajectory toward beating it. Behavioral spine: social proof + curiosity gap (Loewenstein 1994), goal gradient (Hull · Duolingo), implementation intentions (Gollwitzer 1999). Steals from Klaviyo Benchmarks, Strava goal-line, Duolingo streaks.

8.A
Industry Benchmark Trajectory
Each chart shows industry median in gray, your dotted trajectory rising from below to above. Pulsing dot lands in the win zone. Streak counter and 3-send plan turn empty widgets into a beat-the-industry game.
Tactic: Goal gradient + curiosity gap
→
8.B
Klaviyo-Style Benchmark Tiles + If-Then Plans
Each metric becomes a percentile tile vs peers. Bottom-quartile metrics get a specific implementation intention ("If you send Tuesdays at 10 AM, then..."). One celebratory tile reinforces what's working.
Tactic: Peer comparison + implementation intentions
